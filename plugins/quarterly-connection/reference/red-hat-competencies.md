# Red Hat Engineering Competencies - Quick Reference
**Source:** Software Engineer (IC) Progression v10.6

## Two Main Categories

### 1. Responsibilities (What Engineers DO)
Core activities and functions performed by software engineers:

1. **Technical Impact** - Design and develop software solutions across subsystems
2. **Ensure Software Quality and Reliability** - Testing, debugging, and quality practices
3. **Internal and External Collaboration** - Community engagement and cross-functional teamwork
4. **Mentor and Develop Engineering Talent** - Coaching and mentorship activities
5. **Own and Deliver Business Impact** - Business value delivery and ownership
6. **Apply and Advance Technical Practices** - Adoption of new tools and technologies
7. **Leverage and Utilize AI Tools** - Application of AI assistants and agentic workflows *(NEW in v10.6)*
8. **SDLC** - Software Development Life Cycle adherence and improvement

### 2. Job Skills (Capabilities Engineers HAVE)
Foundational skills necessary to effectively perform engineering work:

1. **Technical Acumen** - Proficiency with codebase, tools, and tech stack
2. **Quality Management** - Accountability for quality and testing frameworks
3. **System Design** - Design and architecture capabilities
4. **Communication** - Technical communication to diverse audiences
5. **Collaboration** - Cross-functional and cross-team collaboration
6. **Leadership** - Technical leadership and influence
7. **Business impact** - Understanding of how technical work creates business value
8. **Continuous Learning** - Staying current with technologies and trends
9. **Influence** - Ability to persuade and drive technical direction
10. **Knowledge Sharing** - Documentation, presentations, and teaching

## Bullet Pattern with v10.6 Competencies

**Pattern:**
```text
[Action verb] [deliverable] [readable links] via [RESPONSIBILITY] and [SKILL] to [impact], [how/details].
```

**Key requirements:**
- **Primary competency:** Choose 1 RESPONSIBILITY (what you DID)
- **Secondary competency:** Choose 1 SKILL (capability you DEMONSTRATED)
- Always use exact framework names (case-sensitive)
- Example: "via Internal and External Collaboration and Technical Acumen"

**Why both?**
- Responsibilities alone are too broad ("Technical Impact" applies to almost everything)
- Skills alone miss the action component ("Technical Acumen" doesn't say what you did)
- Combined = specific, meaningful competency demonstration

**This tool MUST NEVER:**
- ❌ Evaluate whether accomplishments demonstrate work at a specific level
- ❌ Assess promotion readiness or role fit
- ❌ Make statements like "this shows PSE-level work"
- ❌ Compare accomplishments to level expectations
- ❌ Suggest what role someone deserves

**This tool ONLY:**

## Level Indicators (for context only - NOT for evaluation)

### Software Engineer (SE)
- **Responsibility scope:** Independent contributions to a subsystem or component
- **Skill level:** Moderate complexity features, applies best practices
- **Collaboration:** Proactive with peers on the team
- **Example:** "via Technical Impact and Technical Acumen to deliver subsystem features independently"

### Senior Software Engineer (SSE)
- **Responsibility scope:** Leads design of subsystem or component
- **Skill level:** Complex features, owns quality, mentors others
- **Collaboration:** Fosters collaborative environment within team
- **Example:** "via Ensure Software Quality and Reliability and Leadership to establish team testing standards"

### Principal Software Engineer (PSE)
- **Responsibility scope:** Leads features across multiple subsystems or components
- **Skill level:** Expert in multiple tech stack areas, builds core test automation
- **Collaboration:** Strong cross-functional relationships across teams
- **Example:** "via Internal and External Collaboration and Influence to drive roadmap decisions across teams"

### Senior Principal Software Engineer (SPSE)
- **Responsibility scope:** Drives technical strategy across multiple subsystems
- **Skill level:** Leads large-scale systems, evolves test automation org-wide
- **Collaboration:** Collaborates with senior leadership, connects with customers
- **Example:** "via Own and Deliver Business Impact and Leadership to define multi-team technical strategy"

### Distinguished Engineer (DE)
- **Responsibility scope:** Drives technical vision with strategic BU impact
- **Skill level:** Industry-recognized expert, creates next-gen test automation
- **Collaboration:** Builds alignment across organization, advises leadership
- **Example:** "via Apply and Advance Technical Practices and Influence to establish BU technology roadmaps"

### Senior Distinguished Engineer (SDE)
- **Responsibility scope:** Sets portfolio strategy with cross-BU impact
- **Skill level:** Industry authority, redefines development methodologies
- **Collaboration:** Executive advisor, shapes industry direction
- **Example:** "via Internal and External Collaboration and Knowledge Sharing to influence portfolio direction and industry standards"

## Key Terms (from Framework)

**Subsystem** - Major technical area involving multiple components (e.g., storage, networking)

**Component** - Isolated, encapsulated unit of software (e.g., adapter, plugin, package)

**Technical Area** - Major project or collection of related projects

**Business Unit (BU)** - Organizational segment with product ownership

**Portfolio** - Set of related products spanning business units

**Cross-functional** - Collaborating across engineering and non-engineering (QE, Docs, PM, CEE)

## Usage Notes

1. **Every bullet needs both:** Responsibility (action) + Skill (capability demonstrated)
2. **Match work type to competency:** Use mapping guide to identify appropriate combination
3. **Be specific:** Use exact framework terms, not interpretations or generic alternatives
4. **Quantify impact:** Include numbers, percentages, team sizes, version ranges
5. **One competency pair per bullet:** Don't try to demonstrate 3+ competencies in a single bullet
6. **v10.6 is current:** All new accomplishment bullets should use v10.6 terminology
